About me

Dr. Mewcha Amha Gebremedhin is a hydrologist, remote sensing expert, and environmental data scientist whose work integrates satellite observations, in-situ measurements, geospatial analysis, and hydrological modeling to improve water-resource assessment in data-scarce and coastal environments. His research addresses rainfall, evapotranspiration, interception loss, surface-groundwater interactions, groundwater sustainability, climate-change impacts, salinization, and nature-based solutions.

He is especially interested in hydroinformatics, including the application of artificial intelligence, machine learning, scientific computing, and data-driven modeling in hydrology. His current work explores how AI, machine learning, and large language models can support groundwater-climate resilience, ecohydrology, environmental decision making, and accessible hydrologic information systems.

Research experience and interests

My research combines hydrology, hydroinformatics, remote sensing, GIS, environmental data science, and AI-assisted modeling to improve water-resource understanding and decision support.

  • Hydroinformatics, AI, and machine learning applications in hydrology
  • Large language models for groundwater and climate-data access
  • Surface-groundwater interaction and groundwater sustainability
  • Climate-change impacts on groundwater, salinity, and coastal water resources
  • Remote sensing of rainfall, evapotranspiration, and interception loss
  • GIS, environmental data science, and reproducible geospatial workflows
  • Nature-based solutions for resilient coastal communities
